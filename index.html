How False Perceptions About Social Media Activism Hurt Meaningful Climate Action

By EcoEcho: Elisa, Aanandita, Nikita, Agrima

Context

Many individuals currently fear that social media merely perpetuates negative and sensationalized content. EcoEcho investigated whether this assumption holds true, or if social media algorithms actually helped disseminate positive updates that encourage climate activists to persist in their efforts against climate change. Our findings reveal that while some believe social media solely amplifies negative sentiments, it can, in fact, inspire climate activists. Thus, this project seeks to demonstrate the positive impact of social media on climate action to our peers and social media platforms. We hope that this project emphasises that social media does not solely propagate negative groupthink, in terms of climate change and more generally.
